Rattle — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 29, 2026

Short version: Rattle listens through your microphone only to recognize the sound of your pill bottle, and that listening happens entirely on your phone. Nothing is ever uploaded, shared, or sold. There are no accounts, no ads, and no tracking.

What Rattle accesses

To let you dismiss an alarm by shaking your real pill bottle, Rattle needs the microphone. When you enroll your bottle, and when an alarm is ringing, Rattle analyzes the incoming sound on your device to detect your bottle's distinctive rattle.

What Rattle stores

Rattle saves a small mathematical "fingerprint" of your bottle's sound and your alarm settings (times, labels) in the app's private storage on your device. Raw audio is processed in the moment and is not retained. This data never leaves your phone, and is removed if you uninstall the app or clear its data.

What Rattle does NOT do

  • It does not record, save, or transmit your audio anywhere.
  • It does not collect, sell, or share any personal data.
  • It has no user accounts, sign-in, ads, analytics, or third-party SDKs.
  • It does not require an internet connection and works fully offline.

Permissions

Microphone (recognize your bottle), notifications and full-screen alarm display, vibration, wake lock, and re-arming alarms after a reboot. Each is used only to make the alarm work.

Children

Rattle is a general-audience utility and is not directed at children under 13.
